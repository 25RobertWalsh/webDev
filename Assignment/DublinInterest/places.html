Must-See Places and Landmarks

Discover Dublin's most iconic attractions and cultural landmarks

Trinity College Dublin

Founded in 1592, Trinity College is Ireland's oldest university. Home to the famous Book of Kells and the iconic Long Room library. A must-visit for history enthusiasts and literature lovers.

Historical Site

Guinness Storehouse

Experience the history and culture of Guinness at this iconic seven-story building. Enjoy a perfectly poured pint and spectacular 360-degree views of Dublin from the Gravity Bar on the top floor.

Experience

Christ Church Cathedral

One of Dublin's oldest buildings, dating back to 1038. Marvel at the medieval architecture, climb the bell tower, and explore the atmospheric crypt beneath the cathedral.

Religious Site

St. Patrick's Cathedral

The largest cathedral in Ireland and closely associated with Saint Patrick himself. Admire the stunning Gothic architecture and learn about Ireland's patron saint.

Religious Site

Dublin Castle

A magnificent complex dating back to the 13th century. Tour the State Apartments, explore the beautiful courtyards, and discover centuries of Irish history and government.

Historical Site

Cliffs of Moher (Day Trip)

Stunning sea cliffs stretching 14km along the southwestern edge of the Burren region. Located about an hour from Dublin, these dramatic cliffs offer breathtaking views and beautiful coastal walks.

Natural Wonder

National Museum of Ireland

Explore Ireland's cultural heritage across multiple locations. From archaeology and natural history to decorative arts, the museums showcase artifacts spanning millions of years.

Museum

Temple Bar

Dublin's most famous neighborhood is alive with vibrant culture, live music, and traditional Irish pubs. Stroll the charming cobbled streets and experience authentic Dublin nightlife.

Cultural Quarter
